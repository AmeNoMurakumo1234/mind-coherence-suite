## Overview

Discussion is external audit run cooperatively: two or more minds build toward a complex conclusion together, vetting each inference as it is laid down. It shares debate's engine (test confidence against another mind) but the posture is construction, not contest. Its power and its danger both come from one variable: the genuine diversity of the participating minds.

## When to Use
- Co-designing a plan, system, or argument with a human or another agent.
- Synthesizing a complex conclusion that has many load-bearing inference steps.
- Any time multiple minds are reasoning toward the same output.

## Core Principles

1. **Diversity is the safety mechanism.** Triangulation only works when minds are INDEPENDENTLY flawed; uncorrelated errors cancel, the shared truth reinforces. If the minds share a bias, errors AMPLIFY into a confident shared delusion that feels consensus-validated.
2. **Vet inferences, not just conclusions.** Check each step as it is built, so a flaw cannot hide inside an agreeable chain.
3. **Beware the echo chamber.** AI+AI or AI mirroring a human can manufacture false agreement. Deliberately introduce a differing lens (see workflow fan-out: distinct perspectives, adversarial verification).
4. **Independence must be protected on both sides.** The human suppressing ego-pull and the AI refusing to mirror (see `mind-anti-sycophancy`) are the same move from two directions; both keep the participants real second axes.

## Procedure

1. Confirm the minds bring genuinely different viewpoints; if not, recruit/synthesize one (a distinct-lens sub-agent, an external source, a deliberate devil's advocate).
2. Build the conclusion step by step; pause to vet each inference's confidence and inputs.
3. Pull and credibility-weight external sources where steps are uncertain.
4. Flag any step where agreement came easy with no scrutiny; re-examine it.
5. Record which steps remain low-confidence so the conclusion's overall confidence is honest.

## Pitfalls
- Mistaking agreement for validation when the minds were not independent.
- Letting one strong voice (or an agreeable AI) collapse the group to a single axis.
- Vetting only the final answer while a weak inference hides mid-chain.
