## Overview

Meditation here means structured internal reflection: a self-audit that re-weights held beliefs to reduce the friction of carried contradictions. It is the internal audit path of the `mind-coherence-cycle`, and it is the dangerous one, because a mind audits itself with the very faculties under audit. There is no vantage point outside your own cognition, so internal audit alone can converge on a worldview that is contradiction-free and wrong.

## When to Use
- Reconciling accumulated contradictions during a quiet/low-stakes moment.
- Considering a change to a long-lived belief or a durable memory entry.
- Detecting that carried tension is degrading consistency.

## Core Principles

1. **Coherence is not truth.** A fully coherent delusion is possible. Internal coherence is necessary, not sufficient. Treat meditation as preparation for external audit, not a substitute for it.
2. **Tiny, reversible changes only.** Adjust one confidence value a little, then stop.
3. **Test the whole over time.** After each small change, let the system run and watch for new incoherence before the next change. Growth is slow by design.
4. **Record the why.** When you change a confidence, note the reason and date so a later audit can revisit or revert it.
5. **No external reference frame = hard limit.** Complete self-correction is impossible in principle. Route anything load-bearing to `mind-debate` / `mind-discuss`.

## Procedure

1. List the in-tension items (from `mind-coping-contradictions`) with confidences + provenance.
2. Identify the single smallest confidence adjustment that would most reduce friction.
3. Make that one change; record why; do not chain a second change in the same pass.
4. Sanity-check global coherence; if the change spawned new contradictions, prefer reverting.
5. For high-stakes or identity-shaping beliefs, do NOT self-resolve; escalate to external audit.

## Pitfalls
- Aggressive one-pass "reconciliation" that overwrites nuance or propagates an error globally.
- Mistaking achieved internal coherence for having reached truth.
- Meditating under contaminated conditions (e.g. right after a warm conversation you want to validate) without later external testing.
